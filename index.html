About Me

It has always been a dream of mine to fly. Initially, this led to an interest in becoming an airline pilot, a passion I maintain through flight simulation and deep engagement with the aviation industry.

Over time, my curiosity matured into the goal of entering the most competitive engineering track in Portugal, with a focus on Aerospace Engineering at Instituto Superior Técnico (IST). After achieving an average grade of 19.13/20, I moved from a rural village to Lisbon to start my degree—a challenge I embraced and overcame.

During my studies, I undertook an internship with the Portuguese Civil Aviation Authority (ANAC), where I developed software predicting UAS trajectories after critical failures. The project contributed to regulatory processes and gave me my first experience of making meaningful contributions in aerospace.

I also completed an Erasmus semester at the Technical University of Munich, focusing on aircraft propulsion and systems engineering. I joined BLUE, a student project dedicated to designing micro turbojets for UAVs, further enhancing my hands-on experience in aerodynamics and propulsion.
